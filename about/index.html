Hola 👋🏼

I was born in Guadalajara, Mexico, and moved to Barcelona in my twenties. Barcelona's home now, but some part of me is always halfway back to a taco stand in Guadalajara. I'm fluent in Spanish and English, get by in French and Catalan, and I've never quite stopped noticing how much gets lost, or found, in the space between languages.

I started out as an advertising copywriter in Mexico. The real shift came after I moved to Barcelona, when I made the jump into content design.

Early at King, a designer stopped me mid-sentence while I was writing an onboarding flow the way I used to write ads: one message, dramatic, precious. He looked at what I'd written and said something like, if you want to tell a story, go write a book. This isn't a book. It's a flow. That's the moment I fell in love with product design. I stopped treating words like a finished product and started treating them like part of a system, one that guides people through an experience: where they came from, where they're headed, and what they need to get done at each step along the way.

A few years in, I realized I didn't know anyone else doing this work in Spain, so I started Content Design España. Seven years, a lot of mentorships and workshops, and no shortage of job referrals later, it's still one of the things I'm proudest of. Not because I built something, but because a bunch of people stopped feeling like the only content designer in the room.

Along the way, I've worked with teams across Europe, the US, Canada, and Latin America, and with people from all sorts of backgrounds and cultures. It's taught me empathy not just for the people I design for, but for the people I design with too.

These days, there's one problem space I keep getting pulled into. Good content design comes from the judgment and taste that experience gives you, not tone of voice guidelines. So I've been building content systems and AI agents that try to carry that forward instead of just enforcing rules. Still figuring it out, but it feels like the right problem to be working on.

Outside of work, I genuinely enjoy teaching, chase the best tacos in town (DM me your faves), and shoot photos on film cameras older than some of my students. When I'm not doing any of that, you'll probably find me at crossfit or out on a trail run.
